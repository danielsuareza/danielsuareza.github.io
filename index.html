Daniel Suarez Aguirre

First-year PhD student, H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering, Georgia Tech.

I am a first-year Operations Research PhD student at Georgia Tech ISyE, advised by Prof. Devansh Jalota. My research is on sequential decision-making under uncertainty, currently focused on online learning for electricity market equilibrium when storage assets act as strategic, intertemporal players.

Before Georgia Tech, I completed a BS at Rice University with majors in Operations Research and Mathematical Economics, and a minor in Financial Computation & Modeling.

research

Current. With Prof. Jalota, I study online learning for equilibrium pricing in electricity markets with strategic battery storage agents. Batteries carry state (state-of-charge) and behave as forward-looking, profit-maximizing players, which breaks standard bandit reductions. The goal is to characterize when a market operator can learn prices online that induce efficient dispatch — work that requires both OR tools (value-function structure of storage, LP duality) and CS/AI tools (regret minimization, contextual bandits).

Past. As an undergraduate at Rice, I worked with Prof. Andrew Schaefer on a Markov Decision Process framework for adaptive radiation therapy, balancing clinical benefit and re-planning cost in head-and-neck cancer treatment. The resulting paper appeared in Physics and Imaging in Radiation Oncology. I have also worked on multi-stage stochastic programming for decentralized wastewater infrastructure, and on Frank–Wolfe algorithms for CNN pruning.
